Nelson Mandela

Here's a time line of Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela:

  • 1918 - On July 7, 1918, Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela is born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944 - Nelson Mandela Joins the African National Congress (ANC).
    With others, Mandela founds the African National Congress Youth League (ANCYL).
  • 1948 - Mandela is elected as National Secretary of the ANCYL.
  • 1952 - Launch of the “Defiance” Campaign, a massive civil disobedience campaign against unjust laws. Mandela is elected National Volunteer-in-Chief for the campaign.
  • 1956 - Mandela is one of 156 accused in the Treason Trial.
  • 1961 - The ANC’s armed movement, Umkhonto we Sizwe (“Spear of the Nation”), is formed with Mandela as commander-in-chief.
  • 1962 - Mandela travels to other parts of Africa and Europe.
  • 1963 - Mandela arrested for illegal exit from the country and incitement to strike. He is convicted and sentenced to five years imprisonment.
  • 1964 - Mandela is sentenced to life imprisonment and sent to Robben Island (later moved to Pollsmoor Prison and then Victor Verster Prison).
  • 1990 - After 27 years in prison, Nelson Mandela is released.
